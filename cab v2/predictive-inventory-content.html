Slide 3

The Problem with Inventory Counting Today

Most hospitality businesses count inventory the same way they always have. The process is familiar, but the cost of that familiarity is hidden.

Effort Without Direction

Your teams count every line on a fixed schedule. A stable, low-value ambient item gets the same count frequency as a high-risk, high-value chilled protein. The result is that 40%+ of counting effort is spent on items with little or no likelihood of variance. That is labour redirected from service, prep, and customer-facing tasks.

Variance Found Too Late

Without prioritisation, the items most likely to have stock discrepancies are not counted any sooner than items that rarely move. By the time variance is found, it has already affected availability (customers see gaps on shelves), waste (stock has expired or been over-ordered), or your financial position (period-end adjustments come as surprises).

No Way to Adapt

Every site, every category, every item follows the same counting pattern. Managers cannot easily say 'count chilled more often' or 'skip low-value ambient this week' without changing the entire process. There is no mechanism to target effort based on what the business actually needs right now.

No Learning, No Improvement

The results of one counting cycle do not inform the next. If an item had zero variance five cycles in a row, it still gets counted on the same schedule. If another item has had variance every single cycle, it gets no extra attention. The process does not learn, so it never gets more efficient.

Slide 6

The Operational Workflow

How a counting cycle runs from start to finish with Predictive Inventory.

1
Data In

Sales, stock, deliveries, waste, and count history feed continuously into the AI model.

2
AI Analyses

The model scores every item by risk, value, rate of sale, count freshness, and variance history.

3
Checks Pushed

Prioritised line checks are sent to operators in the Counting App, with reasons for each.

4
Operator Counts

Teams count only flagged items. Results are captured immediately.

5
Results + Learn

Variance is shown as financial exposure. Outcomes improve the next cycle.

Continuous Learning Loop

Every count result feeds back into Step 1. The model improves each cycle. Items that had no variance are deprioritised. Items with repeated issues are escalated. The system gets smarter the more your teams use it.

The physical counting process does not change. What changes is what gets counted and why.

Slide 8

What Managers See in Fourth iQ

A single console designed for area and regional managers, with three views.

Results

What was recommended, what was counted, what happened. Every item is expandable. Click to see why it was flagged, the variance calculation (theoretical stock vs actual count vs financial exposure), and for uncounted items, an AI estimate of likely variance with a confidence score. Filter by status: all recommended, counted, not counted, or variance found.

Controls

Set the rules for what gets counted. Toggle categories on or off. Set minimum item value, rate of sale, and risk level thresholds per category. Set how many days between counts. Create custom override rules like 'always flag high-value chilled items' or 'skip low-ROS ambient.' A live preview shows exactly which items would be affected before you apply any change.

Performance

Is the system working? Prediction accuracy by category. Data quality health checks. Operator adoption and understanding metrics. This is how you track value and identify where to adjust.

Why Every Item Shows Its Reason

Trust is built on transparency. Here is what your teams see for every flagged item.

Example: Chicken Breast 500g

Chilled Proteins | High Risk | ROS 42/wk | £4.80

  • Risk: High
  • Item value: £4.80
  • Rate of sale: 42/wk
  • Last counted: 6d ago
  • On promotion: Yes
  • Variance: 100 expected − 88 counted = 12 units (12%) × £4.80 = £58 exposure
  • Outcome: Corrected. Stock position adjusted.

Why This Matters

  • Operators who understand why they are counting something are more likely to do it accurately
  • Managers can verify the system is making sensible decisions without checking every item
  • When variance is shown as currency, the business impact is immediately clear
  • If the system flags something questionable, operators can see the reasoning and challenge it
Slide 10

You Control the Rules

The system works to your standards, not a default. Here is what you can configure.

Categories

Toggle which product categories are included in AI checks. Chilled and bakery might be active while ambient stays off.

Risk Levels

Choose which risk levels trigger checks per category. High only, High + Medium, or all three.

Value Threshold

Set a minimum item value. Below this, items are not flagged regardless of risk.

Rate of Sale

Set a minimum ROS. Low-selling items that rarely move can be excluded.

Count Frequency

Set maximum days between counts per category. Chilled might be every 3 days, frozen every 7.

Custom Overrides

Create named rules: 'Always flag promo items' or 'Never flag low-ROS ambient.' Overrides take precedence.

A live preview shows exactly which items would be affected before you apply any change. Nothing changes until you confirm.
